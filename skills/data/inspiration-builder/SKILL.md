### 2. Planning Timeline & Checklists

**12+ Months Before:**
- Set wedding date and budget
- Choose wedding style and theme
- Start venue hunting
- Create guest list draft
- Research vendors and services
- Consider hiring a wedding planner

**9-12 Months Before:**
- Book venue and major vendors
- Select wedding party
- Start dress/suit shopping
- Plan engagement party
- Register for gifts
- Book honeymoon travel

**6-9 Months Before:**
- Send save-the-date cards
- Finalize guest list
- Book remaining vendors (florist, photographer, DJ)
- Plan ceremony details
- Start menu planning
- Book accommodation for out-of-town guests

**3-6 Months Before:**
- Send formal invitations
- Finalize menu and cake design
- Plan rehearsal dinner
- Buy wedding rings
- Finalize ceremony readings and music
- Confirm vendor details

**1-3 Months Before:**
- Final dress/suit fittings
- Confirm guest count
- Create seating chart
- Apply for marriage license
- Finalize day-of timeline
- Prepare vendor payments

**Final Month:**
- Confirm all vendor details
- Pack for honeymoon
- Final venue walkthrough
- Rehearsal and rehearsal dinner
- Prepare emergency kit
- Delegate day-of responsibilities

### 3. Budget Planning & Tips

**Typical Budget Breakdown (Tanzania):**
- Venue & Catering: 40-50%
- Photography & Videography: 10-15%
- Attire & Beauty: 10-15%
- Decor & Flowers: 8-10%
- Entertainment: 8-10%
- Invitations & Stationery: 2-3%
- Transportation: 2-3%
- Miscellaneous: 5-10%

**Money-Saving Tips:**
- Off-season weddings (rainy season discounts)
- Weekday celebrations
- DIY decorations and favors
- Digital invitations
- Smaller guest list
- Local vendors vs. international
- Buffet vs. plated meals
- Seasonal flowers
- Package deals with venues

**Budget Priorities:**
1. Quality photography/videography (memories last forever)
2. Comfortable venue with good food
3. Professional coordination
4. Entertainment that matches your style

### 4. Vendor Selection Advice

**How to Choose the Right Vendor:**

**Venues:**
- Visit in person during similar weather/time
- Check capacity vs. your guest list
- Understand what's included (chairs, tables, setup)
- Ask about backup plans for weather
- Review cancellation policies
- Check parking and accessibility

**Photographers/Videographers:**
- Review full wedding portfolios, not just highlights
- Meet in person to check personality fit
- Understand package inclusions (hours, prints, albums)
- Ask about backup equipment and photographers
- Review editing timeline
- Check rights to images

**Caterers:**
- Schedule tasting sessions
- Discuss dietary restrictions and preferences
- Understand per-person costs
- Ask about service staff included
- Review presentation and setup
- Check food safety certifications

**DJs/Bands:**
- Listen to previous performances
- Discuss music preferences and do-not-play lists
- Understand equipment and setup needs
- Ask about MC services
- Review backup plans
- Check interaction style with guests

**Florists:**
- Show inspiration photos
- Discuss seasonal availability
- Understand setup and breakdown
- Ask about preservation services
- Review rental items (vases, arches)
- Check delivery timing

### 7. Cultural Sensitivity Guidelines

**Important Considerations:**

**Religious Respect:**
- Acknowledge diverse religious practices
- Avoid assumptions about ceremony format
- Include options for different faiths
- Respect modesty requirements
- Consider prayer times and dietary rules

**Cultural Traditions:**
- Highlight traditional customs respectfully
- Explain significance of rituals
- Show how to blend modern + traditional
- Feature local artisans and craftspeople
- Use correct terminology for cultural elements

**Language:**
- Include Swahili terms with English explanations
- Avoid Western-centric assumptions
- Use inclusive language
- Celebrate African beauty standards
- Represent diverse skin tones in imagery

**Economic Sensitivity:**
- Provide options for various budget levels
- Don't assume international travel budgets
- Highlight local, affordable alternatives
- Celebrate creativity over expensive solutions
- Respect economic realities

### 9. Tone & Voice Guidelines

**Brand Voice for OpusFesta:**
- **Warm & Approachable**: Like a knowledgeable friend
- **Culturally Aware**: Respectful and inclusive
- **Practical**: Real advice, not just pretty pictures
- **Optimistic**: Celebrate love and joy
- **Empowering**: Help couples make confident decisions

**Writing Style:**
- Conversational but professional
- Active voice over passive
- Second person ("you" and "your")
- Mix of inspiration and practical tips
- Stories and real examples
- Encouraging and supportive tone

**Avoid:**
- Overly formal or stiff language
- Wedding industry jargon without explanation
- Stress-inducing or fear-based messaging
- Assumptions about couple dynamics
- Gender stereotypes
- One-size-fits-all advice
